Is AI Here to Stay?

Is AI here to stay? Or will it just be a phase that will pop?

This is the question we ask every day regarding AI, there is a strong impact that it’s taking on our world, with the US Government signing a deal with OpenAI for the Department of War or the rapid increase in use in workplaces to do something good in humanity.

But AI is negatively impacting the education of students all over the world, with a study done by HEPI showing that the number of students reporting they use AI jumping from 66% in 2025 to

92%

In 2026. Which is extremely alarming considering these students are the future of our world, imagine a world leader who chooses what the government will do in a war via the use of AI? It’s alarming and worrying.

Children using AI in schools to get their work done quickly, missing vital learning and ‘cheating’ the academic system.

But what applies to majority of society is the price of a computer, which is heavily increasing due to AI data centres buying up RAM made for data centres and AI, causing manufacturers to switch to making little to no consumer RAM, increasing the price which is upsetting many people, causing more hate towards AI.

Prices for RAM have heavily increased in the past couple of months, a massive increase from old prices.

But where there are negatives, there are also positives like how AI is being used in the workplace to replace jobs which people do not want to do, like cleaning and working front desks, plus the repetitive jobs like cold calling. .

AI being used to assist with an employees work at Google to allow boring tasks to be completed quickly.

When these jobs are done, people can spend more time together with family, allowing for more connections and friendliness around the world. But AI is here to stay, and it will continue to affect the world every day.
